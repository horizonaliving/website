About Horizon Assisted Living · South Minneapolis

We keep
a home,

Everything we do at Horizon flows from that difference. Our pace is unhurried, our scale is small, and the care is built around the person — never the other way around.

Our Philosophy

Care, slow enough to feel.

Most assisted living in Minnesota operates at scale — sixty, ninety, a hundred residents behind a single front desk. That's not wrong, but it's not us. Horizon is a small, licensed residential home with space for five. A single house, a single team, a single dining table. The kind of place where morning coffee isn't on a schedule — it's a habit.

This scale isn't an accident. It's the whole point. When staff know five residents personally, they catch the quiet things — a slower walk, a skipped meal, a changed mood — that bigger places often miss until they become crises. Small isn't cozy marketing. It's a care model.

A Day at Horizon

What daily life feels like.

There's no strict schedule at Horizon — only a gentle rhythm. Residents wake, eat, walk, rest, and gather at their own pace, with staff present for whatever each moment asks for.

Morning

A warm breakfast, coffee, the radio or a newspaper. Medications and morning routines handled at each resident's pace. A walk to the porch if the weather is kind.

Midday

Lunch together at the table. Staff-led activities if anyone wants them — music, reading aloud, a card game. Quiet time for those who'd rather rest. Appointments attended as needed.

Evening

A family-style dinner. A quiet hour in the living room — a book, a show, a conversation. Nighttime routines and medications on each resident's schedule. Awake staff on overnight watch, every night.

Why Small-Home Care

The difference you feel on day one.

Large assisted living facilities and small residential homes like Horizon are both legitimate care models. Here's what's different about ours — and why it matters for families making a big decision.

01

Familiar Faces

With five residents and a consistent staff team, your loved one is cared for by the same people — not rotating faces. Trust builds. So does safety, because staff notice small changes.

02

A Real Household

Meals from a home kitchen. A shared dining table. A living room that looks like one. No corridors, no wings, no institutional lighting. Just a house that happens to offer care.

03

Individual Pace

Without a large group to synchronize, residents wake, eat, and move at their own rhythm. Care happens around each person's day — not the other way around.

04

Direct Communication

Families call and reach someone who actually knows their loved one. Not a switchboard, not a charge nurse they've never met. Small means accessible.

05

Neighborhood Life

3136 Bloomington Ave S sits in a real South Minneapolis neighborhood — walks, local shops, changing seasons. Being in a community, not apart from one, matters for wellbeing.

06

Same Care, Less Noise

All the licensed services, medication management, and 24/7 staffing you'd find at a larger facility — just without the institutional scale that can make assisted living feel impersonal.
